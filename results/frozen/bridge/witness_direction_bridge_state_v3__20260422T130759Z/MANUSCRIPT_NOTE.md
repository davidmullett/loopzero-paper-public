# Manuscript-facing witness-direction note

## Results-ready sentence
Directional witness summaries were fully aligned on the canonical recommender benchmark
and on the canonical public-markets benchmark when markets were summarized over the
last 30 minutes of each exact canonical unit. In a wider 60-minute markets summary,
gain `G` and recursive persistence `p` remained directionally aligned whereas
diversity-change `delta_change` weakened.

## Short interpretation
The recommender bridge is fully aligned on its canonical benchmark. The markets bridge
is also fully aligned under a localized 30-minute late-window summary and is therefore
best interpreted as strongest in the late-window regime rather than uniformly invariant
across wider summary windows.

## Caption-ready version
Witness-direction measurement on the canonical markets and recommender benchmarks.
On the canonical recommender benchmark, pre-collapse event units showed higher gain `G`,
higher recursive persistence `p`, and lower diversity `delta` than reference controls.
On the canonical public-markets benchmark, the same directional pattern was recovered
when markets were summarized over the last 30 minutes of each exact canonical unit
using `G`, `p`, and diversity-change `delta_change`. In a wider 60-minute markets
summary, `G` and `p` remained directionally aligned whereas `delta_change` weakened.
