# Adapter Public Notes

This note describes the public-facing role of the domain adapter layer in the research implementation associated with the manuscript.

The published research package uses domain-specific operationalizations to map native telemetry into three common witness roles:

- **diversity**: a publication-facing summary of breadth of realized system state,
- **self-reinforcement**: a publication-facing summary of endogenous recycling of prior outputs,
- **gain**: a publication-facing summary of amplification of perturbation or influence.

These witness roles are used to instantiate the theorem-to-telemetry bridge described in the manuscript across heterogeneous recursive domains.

Representative public interpretation by domain family:

- **adaptive decision systems**: action or policy diversity, endogenous reinforcement of prior allocations, and amplification of local preference shifts;
- **recommender systems**: content or interaction diversity, recycling of recommendation-induced engagement, and amplification of preference signals;
- **markets**: order-flow or participation diversity, endogenous reinforcement of local market behavior, and amplification of return-impact structure;
- **recursive model replay**: breadth of replay/training state, self-contamination or replay fraction, and amplification across recursive updates;
- **cognition**: breadth of response or behavioral state, endogenous replay or perseveration, and amplification of local perturbations across trials;
- **culture**: breadth of topical state, symbolic recycling, and amplification of recurrent narrative or topic structure.

This note is intentionally public-facing and publication-bounded. It does not disclose production formulas, threshold maps, calibration ladders, tie-break logic, deployment-specific adapter contracts, or other non-public implementation details.
